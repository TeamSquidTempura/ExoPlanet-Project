Potential for Life - Xenobiology

Considering the factor of Goldilocks zone and its planet's atmosphere, The presence of oxygen and trace amounts of methane suggest possible biological activity.

If life exists on Planet T, it likely evolved to have thick layer of fur preventing from losing body temperature caused by planet's low average temperature. Potential lifeforms could range from simple microbial organisms to complex multicellular life, adapted to the slightly higher gravity and similar atmospheric composition to Earth.

Seasonal Variations

With a planet tilt of 23 degrees, Planet T experiences seasonal variations similar to Earth. The shorter orbital period causes seasons to last approximately 16 Earth days each. This predictable seasonal cycle could support diverse and stable ecosystems within various biomes ranging from polar ice caps to tropical regions. However, since orbital period is not as long as Earth, there could be some potential problems that might affect some plants or animals that has been brought from the Earth. It would require some sort of adaptation into that climate condition.

Transportation to the Planet

According to EOL#2, the maximum speed we can possibly travel from Earth is 4.0e+8 km/h. Therefore, it takes approximately 1500 years to reach the planet. The distance between its planet and the Earth is not large compared to the other possibly habitable exoplanets in terms of light-years. However, when we actually have to travel this distance, since we cannot travel in speed of light, it takes thousands of years. Overall, this planet can potentially become our second Earth, however further technology advancement in transportation is required when we actually travel there. Although, we can still have further discoveries using electromagnetic spectrum or other techniques that helps to understand more components of its planet. By then, we will get enough information if this planet is worth going for investigation and exploration to determine its habitability.
